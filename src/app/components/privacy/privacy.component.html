What this is

Xomware is a personal portfolio of small apps run by Dominick Giordano (handle @domgiordano). This page describes what data Xomware collects when you use any app under xomware.com or any sub-app such as xomappetit.xomware.com.

What we collect

  • Email address — required to create an account.
  • Handle (e.g. @domgiordano) — chosen by you at sign-up.
  • Display name and profile picture — if you sign in with Google, we store the name and avatar Google returns. You can change either at any time on the profile page.
  • Sign-in / sign-up timestamps — we keep a small audit log so the operator (Dominick) can see usage. Rows expire automatically after 90 days.
  • App-specific content you create — e.g. recipes you save in Xom Appétit, files you edit, or any other data you enter into a Xomware app.

How we store it

Account data lives in AWS Cognito (identity provider) and AWS DynamoDB (profile + app data), encrypted at rest with AWS KMS. Avatars live in S3 behind CloudFront. Everything is hosted in us-east-1.

Who we share it with

Nobody. Xomware does not sell, rent, or share your data. The only third parties that touch your data are AWS (the hosting provider) and, if you choose Google sign-in, Google (purely to authenticate you — we receive your email + display name + avatar URL and nothing else).

Cookies and tracking

Xomware uses session tokens issued by Cognito to keep you signed in. There is no third-party advertising, no cross-site tracking, and no analytics that send your data outside Xomware.

Your rights

You can delete your account at any time from the profile page, which removes your row from the identity pool and your profile data from DynamoDB. App-specific content (recipes, etc.) is removed as part of account deletion. To request a copy of your data or ask any other question, email dominickj.giordano@gmail.com.

Changes

If this policy changes, the “Last updated” date above will change with it. Material changes will also be announced on the home page.
